Extra-Base Performance Benchmarked to Conventional Road

Benchmark = Conventional (0 %). Bars = % change in load-induced Δσ_yy delivered to the subgrade surface. Green = below benchmark (less stress = better); red = above benchmark (worse). Equal applied load in all cases.

Values —

Reading it. Against the Conventional benchmark, thin Extra Bases (5–15 cm) sit above the line (they deliver more stress to their shallow subgrade), 20 cm is essentially on par, and from 25 cm the Extra Base drops below Conventional — reaching −32 % at 50 cm and −37 % at 60 cm. First thickness to beat Conventional ≈ 25 cm; strong cost-effective relief ≈ 50 cm before the curve flattens.
